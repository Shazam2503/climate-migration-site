    Overview

    What Is Climate Migration?

    Climate migration occurs when people are forced to leave their homes due to environmental changes — rising seas, extreme droughts, devastating floods, and crop failure — that make survival impossible. Unlike traditional refugees fleeing persecution, climate migrants often fall outside legal protection frameworks, creating a critical gap in global humanitarian response.

    Why People Are Displaced

    Climate displacement is driven by a set of interconnected environmental stressors — each compounding the other, and hitting the world's most vulnerable populations hardest.

    Human Impact

    The Scale of Displacement

    By 2050, without urgent action, up to 216 million people could be forced to move within their own countries. The burden falls hardest on Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Latin America.
